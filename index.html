Research Interests

My main interests lie in extragalactic astronomy. I like to think about questions like: How do massive galaxies evolve through cosmic time? How do galaxy quench their star formation? How and when do a galaxy's kinematic structure and morpholgy undergo changes?

I also like to think about larger statistical questions involving overall populations of galaxies in the Universe. I have recently finished a study on the environmental dependence of rotational support in massive, quiescent galaxies using the Large Early Galaxy Astrophysics Census (LEGA-C Survey). Now, I am working on testing BAGPIPES to recreate hyper-parameter spaces in preparation for the launch of the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) and the Cosmic Evolution Early Release Science (CEERS) program.

About Me

Just your average dragon-slaying, guitar-playing, gardening astrophysicist. I spend the majority of my free time in the gym, on a soccer field, or taking care of my plants. I also love playing my guitar, World of Warcraft and the occasional DnD campaign with friends.

I also love doing Astronomy-/science-related outreach. I have volunteered as a Physics and Astronomy Department tutor for both University of Pittsburgh students and Greater Pittsburgh Area high school students. Outside of tutoring, I love participating in Astronomy on Tap and I have particpated in multiple Open House/Conference Tour nights at the Allegheny Observatory. I am always willing to sit and talk to people about Astronomy and its related fields so conferences and outreach are some of my favorite things to do.
